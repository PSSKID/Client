What is SSL?

SSL stands for Secure Sockets Layer, a method used to secure and encrypt sensitive information like credit cards, usernames, passwords, and other private data sent over the Internet. Website pages secured with SSL are those branded with the "HTTPS" in their URL address. 

Each ClientHub is hosted on our secure servers utilizing SSL (Secure Socket Layer) encryption to ensure that your customer account information is kept safe and secure and accessible only to your staff and customers as you permit.

This short video provides a more detailed explanation.


Why is SSL encrytion so critically important for your business?

Everyone knows that it is critical you secure any financial data, but even basic personal information such a personâ€™s name, address, phone number and email address should also be secured.

While your ClientHubs will be SSL encrypted, your website should also be fully secured no matter how much or how little information you gather from your visitors.

Google Chrome and other web browsers are already marking sites with "Not Secure" in the address bar when the website does not have an SSL Certificate regardless of whether you are requesting sensitive information.   

During the course of 2018 and beyond, all websites without an SSL Certificate will be marked as not secure simply because they are not secure, and not because they are collecting any sensitive information at all.
